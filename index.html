I — A Letter From Marco’s kitchen
to yours.

Cooking is not a performance. It is a discipline. A trade learned by doing, by repetition, by standing next to someone who knows — and paying attention.

This Academy exists for that reason. Not for celebrity. Not for television. For the craft. For the young cook who walks into a kitchen at six in the morning and wants to be better by eight.

We built it to give apprentices a proper education — the kind I wish had existed when I was starting out. Real standards. Real mentorship. A body of knowledge, written down, that belongs to them for the rest of their career.

Marco Pierre White Founder · The Academy of Culinary Excellence
II — The Programmes

Four standards.
One tradition.

Every apprentice we enrol follows a nationally recognised Skills England standard, mapped to the endpoint assessment, and built around the Marco Pierre White Learning Collection. Progression is the architecture — from the first knife cut at Level 2 to full culinary leadership at Level 4.

I
Level 2 · Foundation

Commis Chef

Standard · ST0228

The first rung. The apprentice learns the bench — knife skills, mise en place, the rhythm of service, the discipline of a working kitchen. They leave with the technical grounding every professional cook needs, and the instinct that cannot be taught from a book alone.

Duration12–18 months
EntryNo prior experience
Funding bandUp to £9,000

II
Level 2 · Volume

Production Chef

Standard · ST0589

For the kitchens where consistency is everything — care homes, hospitals, schools, hotels, branded restaurants. The Production Chef learns to deliver the same plate, the same standard, the same care, a hundred times over. That is a craft of its own.

Duration12–15 months
EntryLive kitchen role
Funding bandUp to £8,000

III
Level 3 · Leadership

Senior Production Chef

Standard · ST0232

The step from cook to leader. Menu planning, team supervision, compliance, cost control, the calm head during the push. This is where an apprentice learns to hold the line — and to stand behind every plate that leaves the pass with their name on it.

Duration15–24 months
EntryL2 or equivalent
Funding bandUp to £15,000

IV
Level 4 · Mastery

Senior Culinary Chef

Standard · ST0864

The most advanced apprenticeship in professional cookery. Full culinary leadership — directing a brigade, developing menus, running a kitchen as a business. At this level, an apprentice is not learning how to cook. They are learning how to run the place.

Duration18–30 months

III — How It Works

Two paths.
One destination.

Whether you’re an employer building a brigade or a cook building a career, the journey follows the same four movements — framed the way you need to see it.

i

Apply

Tell us where you are now and where you want to be. If you’re already in a kitchen, we talk to your employer. If you’re not, we help you find the right one.

ii

Enrol

We match you to the right standard — Level 2 to 4 — and register you formally. You receive your own copy of the Learning Collection on day one.

iii

Train

You learn in a real kitchen, on real service, with a workplace mentor and a dedicated Academy coach. Monthly sessions. Quarterly reviews. Constant feedback.

iv

Qualify

You complete the endpoint assessment — observation, portfolio, professional discussion — and leave with a nationally recognised qualification and a trade that is yours for life.

i

Consult

We visit your kitchen. We understand your service, your team, your needs. We advise on which standards fit which roles — Commis for the new starter, Senior Culinary Chef for your head chef succession plan.

ii

Enrol

We handle the paperwork. Levy draw-down, Skills England registration, learner onboarding. You sign one agreement. Everything else is ours to run.

iii

Deliver

Your team trains on the job. An Academy coach attends your kitchen on a published cycle. Progress is visible, measurable, and reported to you every quarter.

iv

Retain

Apprenticeship is the most powerful retention tool in hospitality. Our cohorts stay in post. They rise through your brigade. You build a kitchen that trains itself.

VI — Funding

Most programmes cost the employer nothing at all.

For levy payers

Fully funded via your Apprenticeship Levy

If your annual payroll exceeds £3 million, you already pay the levy. We draw it down on your behalf — the programme costs you nothing out-of-pocket, and you use money you’ve already contributed.

100%Levy funded
For non-levy payers

95% Government co-investment

Smaller employers pay just 5% of the programme cost — the government pays the remaining 95% directly to us. For apprentices aged 16–21, the employer contribution is often reduced further.

5%Employer contribution

Both routes may qualify for an additional £1,000 employer incentive where the apprentice is 16–18 years old. Our funding team confirms exact figures in writing before you commit to anything.
